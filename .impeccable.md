# VailMorse — Design Context

Locked in 2026-05-25 via `/impeccable teach`. This file is the source of truth
for design decisions. Update it (don't override silently) when direction shifts.

## Users

VailMorse is used by amateur radio operators in a **casual social CW** context.
Users are deep in the hobby's subculture (callsigns, MIDI keyers, repeater
etiquette) and treat the channel like a hangout — closer to a CW-flavored
Discord than a serious station log. Sessions are short, conversational, often
opportunistic rather than scheduled. The app sits in the same mental category
as Slack or Discord for these users, not "ham radio software."

## Brand Personality

Three concrete words: **warm · hand-built · opinionated**.

- **Warm** because the audience is a small hobby community with continuity to
  telegraph history.
- **Hand-built** because operators are tinkerers; the interface should feel
  *made by someone*, not generated.
- **Opinionated** because we'd rather pick a voice and commit than ship default
  iOS chrome.

The emotional goal is *welcome to the shack* — confident, friendly,
period-aware without being costume-y.

## Aesthetic Direction

The app offers **four themes** the operator can pick from. **Quiet utility is
the default** — it lets content lead and stays out of the way for new users.
The other three are committed-voice options.

| Theme | Voice | Scheme | Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Quiet utility** (default) | Reeder / Things lineage. Content-forward. | Light | SF Pro |
| **Spec instrument** | Aviation EFB. Mono numerics, sharp rules, amber accent. | Dark slate | SF Pro + SF Mono |
| **Retro telegraph** | Brass-and-bakelite without costume. Hairlines, oxblood. | Light parchment | New York (serif) |
| **CRT terminal** | Amber phosphor, scanlines, blinking cursor, ASCII voice. | Deep amber dark | SF Mono |

Anti-references — explicitly **not** this:

- Generic iOS chrome (standard tab bar + grouped lists + accent-blue everywhere).
- Costume retro (wood textures, faux dials, ornate borders). Kitsch.
- Crypto / trader neon-on-black. Wrong subculture.

## Design Principles

1. **OperatingView is the headline.** Every other screen earns complexity by
   being task-specific (Skeds, Contacts, Settings) and can keep system chrome.
2. **Theme is voice, not skin.** Each theme commits to typography + structure +
   color + microcopy (e.g., *Strike* vs *HOLD TO TX*). Don't bland the
   differences out by collapsing them to identical labels.
3. **Activity is the hero.** The signal timeline is what makes Vail *Vail*.
   Theme its lanes, not just its background.
4. **System fonts with design hints for v1.** Custom TTFs (Bitter, Spectral,
   VT323) are a polish pass — `.default / .serif / .monospaced` carry 80% of
   the voice without binary bloat.
5. **Fork the HIG intentionally, don't fight it accidentally.** Forcing
   `.preferredColorScheme` per theme is justified because each theme *requires*
   its scheme. Any other appearance override needs the same justification or
   it doesn't happen.

## Implementation

- Theme tokens live in `Sources/Theme/AppTheme.swift` (palette + typography +
  ornament + microcopy).
- `Sources/Theme/ThemeEnvironment.swift` bridges `@AppStorage` choice into the
  SwiftUI environment.
- Views read `@Environment(\.appTheme) var theme` and pull tokens; they don't
  hard-code colors.
- Themed surface area as of 2026-05-25: OperatingView + TouchKeyView +
  SignalTimelineView. Other screens (Contacts, Skeds, Settings) inherit color
  scheme + accent only and stay in system chrome. Roll the rest out in
  separate passes.
